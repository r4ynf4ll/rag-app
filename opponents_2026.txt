TEAM OVERVIEW: Quentin State Cardinals

Record: 5-2 (2-1 conference). Coming off a 24-21 loss at Riverbend Tech (the Cardinals were a 13-point favorite). Two weeks ago they beat Carbondale 38-10 on the road.

Head coach: Bryce Harrowgate (5th season, 31-22 overall). Offensive coordinator: Tyrell Vega (2nd year, came from a Group of Five program). Defensive coordinator: Lonnie Pickering (4th year, longtime NFL position coach).

Offensive scheme: Spread RPO out of 11 personnel as the base look, with frequent shifts to 12 personnel in the red zone. Heavy use of run-pass option triggers off the inside zone — defensive linemen who close hard inside are punished with the bubble screen. Tempo varies; they will sprint to the line after explosive plays and milk the clock after first downs in plus-territory.

Defensive scheme: 4-2-5 with a single-high safety (Cover 3) as the base call. They blitz roughly 28% of passing downs, almost exclusively from the slot/nickel position. The MIKE rarely blitzes. Their tendency is to play press-bail at the boundary and soft off in the slot — a tendency we will exploit.

2024 result against Riverton State (last meeting): Quentin 27, Riverton 24, in Quentin (OT). The Cardinals scored on a 38-yard scramble by their quarterback in overtime. Three of their five sacks that game came from the same edge rusher (DeJesus, who returns this year as a senior).

Three things to know going in:
1. They are 0-2 in games where the opponent rushed 40+ times. Their defensive front wears down. We must commit to the run.
2. Their offense has averaged 18 points fewer on natural grass than on turf this year (small sample, but consistent). We play on grass.
3. Their MIKE linebacker, Coleman, has been playing through a right-shoulder injury since Week 3. Run at his shoulder in pass-pro and on counter plays.

SITUATIONAL TENDENCIES — QUENTIN STATE

3rd-and-short (1-2 yards): 71% run. Almost always inside zone or QB sneak. They have run the same QB sneak 14 times this season and converted 13. The one failure was last week — first time we've seen them blocked into the backfield on it.

3rd-and-medium (3-6 yards): Heavy RPO. The mesh-point read is the cornerstone — if the linebacker triggers run, the QB pulls and throws the bubble; if the linebacker stays, the back keeps and runs inside zone. They've converted 53% on this down-and-distance.

3rd-and-long (7+): 80% pass. They get the ball out quick — average time to throw on third-and-long is 2.31 seconds. Aggressive blitz here pays off; they have struggled against simulated pressure where the rush comes from a non-conventional gap.

Red zone offense: Drops to 12 personnel. Two TEs in the formation. Heavy boundary fade to Banks-Easter on the goal line — five of his six TDs this year are red-zone fades.

Red zone defense: Significantly more man coverage than they play between the 20s. Pickering trusts his corners on islands inside the 10. Crossing routes have hurt them in the red zone — three of the four red-zone TDs they've allowed came on shallow crosses.

Two-minute offense: They have not been good at it. Two two-minute drives with a chance to win or tie this year, both ended in punts or turnovers on downs. Vasquez's clock management has been a flaw.

Late and close: They run the ball when leading by one score in the 4th quarter — 87% run rate. Disciplined. Good clock-eaters.

OPPONENT REPORT: Holden Vasquez (#7)
Position: Quarterback
Class: Junior (transferred from Coffeyville Community College after his freshman season)
Height/Weight: 6'2", 215 lbs
2026 Stats: 1,742 passing yards, 13 TD, 6 INT, 64.1%. 412 rushing yards, 7 TDs.

Background: JUCO transfer who started his college career at Coffeyville (Kansas) before moving to Quentin State as a sophomore. Now in his second year as the starter. Older for his class — turns 23 in November.

How They Win: Athleticism and second-reaction creativity. He extends plays with his legs better than any QB we'll face this season. Strong-armed enough to push the ball outside the numbers, and the read-option keeper is a real threat — three of his rushing TDs are scrambles of 20+ yards.

How to Attack: Confine him to the pocket. Spy on him with our SAM linebacker on obvious passing downs. He locks onto his first read more than his stats suggest — 4 of his 6 INTs were on his first read, attacking the boundary. When pressured up the middle, he drifts right; the right edge can come free if our defensive tackle wins inside.

Key Tendencies: Eyes telegraph the throw. Watches the safety on RPOs to decide give vs. throw — the safety can disguise rotation pre-snap and bait him.

Game Plan Notes: Force him to win from the pocket against pressure. Cover 0 on 3rd-and-7+ has worked against him on tape (two sacks, one INT in those situations).

OPPONENT REPORT: Travonn Maddox (#21)
Position: Running Back (RB1)
Class: Sophomore
Height/Weight: 5'10", 215 lbs
2026 Stats: 712 rushing yards, 8 TDs (5.4 ypc). 18 receptions, 142 yards.

Background: Came in as a top-300 recruit. Took over the starting job in Week 4 after the previous starter went down with an ankle. Has not lost a fumble this year.

How They Win: Contact balance and short-area burst. He is a between-the-tackles bruiser who does not go down on first contact. Runs angry. The Cardinals' play action is more dangerous than the analytics show because the run threat is real.

How to Attack: Outside speed is below average — 4.62 forty. He cannot bounce wide and outrun a sideline. Force him east-west and our linebackers will catch him. Set the edge hard with the SAM and crash the DT inside; he doesn't have a true cutback gear.

Key Tendencies: Lowers his shoulder consistently — he is an arm-tackle nightmare but a wrap-up tackler's dream. Tackle low. He does not protect the ball when reaching for the goal line; we have seen one fumble forced this season at the goal line by another team.

Game Plan Notes: Goal-line strip drill all week. Two-back sets are running plays 78% of the time — this is a tell.

OPPONENT REPORT: Asher Pulaski (#28)
Position: Running Back (RB2 / change-of-pace)
Class: Freshman
Height/Weight: 5'9", 188 lbs
2026 Stats: 215 rushing yards (8.4 ypc), 2 TDs. 22 receptions, 274 yards, 3 TDs. Returns kicks (24.8 avg).

Background: True freshman. Hand-timed 4.34 forty at his recruiting camp — fastest player on the Cardinals' roster.

How They Win: Pure speed in space. He is a screen-game and outside-zone weapon. They use him on jet motion roughly twice per game to stress contain.

How to Attack: Frame is small and he does not break tackles. First-contact tackle = end of play. Don't overrun him on screens — tackle low and to the inside.

Key Tendencies: Almost always lined up offset to the QB, not in a true Pony backfield with Maddox. When both backs are on the field together, screen tendency rises sharply.

Game Plan Notes: Squib kicks and pooched punts to keep the ball out of his hands on returns. Last week the Riverbend Tech kicker out-kicked his coverage to him; Pulaski went 78 yards.

OPPONENT REPORT: Roy Banks-Easter (#11)
Position: Wide Receiver (X)
Class: Junior
Height/Weight: 6'5", 218 lbs
2026 Stats: 41 receptions, 615 yards, 6 TDs.

Background: Two-year starter. Their offense in the red zone is largely an exercise in giving him chances to win contested catches. Five of his six TDs are red-zone fade balls.

How They Win: Catch radius and contested-catch ability. Wins on the goal-line fade and back-shoulder throws. Body shield in the red zone is excellent.

How to Attack: Pure speed is average — 4.58 forty. Off-coverage and a vertical safety help bracket the post. He will not separate cleanly underneath. Press him at the line and physical corners can disrupt his route stem; he has been pressed to the ground twice this year by defenders 6'2"+.

Key Tendencies: Drift-out fade against off-press is his go-to in the red zone. He does not run a quality slant — they don't even call it for him. On 3rd-and-long he runs comebacks 65% of the time.

Game Plan Notes: Allard-Boyle should travel with him for the entire game. Check our pass game on pre-snap motion to help with bracket coverage.

OPPONENT REPORT: Marcus DeJesus (#94)
Position: Edge / DE
Class: Senior
Height/Weight: 6'4", 252 lbs
2026 Stats: 9 sacks (conference leader), 14 TFL.

Background: Conference's leading pass-rusher. Three of his sacks against us last year. Pre-NFL prospect.

How They Win: First-step explosion and a long-arm move that beats slow-to-set tackles. His motor is the best on their defense.

How to Attack: Run at him. He is a one-trick pass-rusher and below-average against the run. Counter and power schemes have gotten 5+ ypc against him this year. Do not leave our right tackle on him in pass-pro on critical downs — chip him with a back, slide a TE.

Key Tendencies: He stunts on the snap when he reads pass; he is not a discipline-first player. Draws and screens have hurt him — he ran himself out of the play three times against Carbondale.

Game Plan Notes: He is the player to neutralize. Game-plan a screen, a draw, and an early run at his side. Force him to play the run on at least 60% of his snaps.

OPPONENT REPORT: Sundiata Coleman (#42)

GAME PLAN SUMMARY — KEYS TO VICTORY

1. Run the football. They are 0-2 against teams with 40+ rushes. Tatum-Reed wears down; Coleman is hurt; the front cannot hold up against tempo. Counter and counter-trey at Coleman's right shoulder are the base calls.

2. Neutralize DeJesus. Chip with a back or TE on every passing down. Run a screen and a draw early in his direction. He is an effort player on the rush; force him to read the run.

3. Put Allard-Boyle on an island and live with it. Travel him with our X. Win elsewhere. Banks-Easter is a red-zone problem we will live with — keep them out of the red zone.

4. Test Heller. Run a deep crosser on the second drive. He is one targeting hit from a long suspension. Force the choice.

5. Force Vasquez to win from the pocket. Spy with the SAM. Bring pressure off the slot blitz to disrupt the RPO. Keep him in front and make him beat us with his arm.

6. Wind matters. Defer if we win the toss; let them kick into the wind in Q1.
