ROSTER SUMMARY — POST-WEEK 7

Twenty key contributors profiled. Of these, seven are seniors who depart after this season (Halberstam, Tatum-Holloway, Pemberton, McKendrick, Mubarak, Bridgeman, Lassiter). The remaining thirteen return.

Four captains in 2026: Halberstam (offense), McKendrick (offense), Mubarak (defense), Bridgeman (defense). All four graduate.

Position groups carrying the most 2027 transition pressure: offensive line (losing the four-year starting LT), linebacker (losing the program's smartest player and a four-year defensive captain), and slot receiver (losing the team's leading receiver). The 2027 recruiting class was structured around these three areas of need.

Strongest 2026 development stories: Crowder (breakout sophomore), Vance (pass-rush expansion), Jefferson (walk-on to scholarship to starter in two years), Pearce (ACL recovery to starting safety).

Players to monitor in the offseason: Cabello vs. Stoltz at center (live competition); Vinter vs. incoming Marsh at kicker (live competition); Donaldson vs. incoming Boyles for the slot speed role.

Overall roster health: 100% available. No players currently on injury reserve. Two minor in-season managed injuries (Halberstam shoulder, Pearce knee maintenance) — both green.
