GRADING SCALE
Recruiting Tier: Blue Chip (top 100 nationally) / Gold (top 500) / Silver (top 1500) / Bronze (priority developmental).
Scout Confidence: High / Medium / Low. Reflects how confident the staff is in the projection — independent of tier. A Silver prospect can carry High confidence; a Blue Chip can carry Low.
Year-1 Projection: Day-One Starter / Rotational / Redshirt / Developmental.
The Mariners signed 20 prospects in this cycle. Reports follow.

PROSPECT REPORT: Marcus Harlan Jr.
Position: Quarterback
Class: High school senior
Recruiting Tier: Blue Chip
Scout Confidence: High
Year-1 Projection: Rotational (Year-2 starter projected)

Measurables: 6'3", 215 lbs. 40 time 4.74. Vertical 32 inches. Hand size 9 7/8 inches.
Hometown: Plano, Texas
High school: Plano West HS — three-year starter, 9,200 career passing yards, 88 TDs.

Marcus is the son of former NFL quarterback Marcus Harlan Sr., who played seven seasons in the league with three teams. His pedigree shows: he runs a pro-style offense with command rare for his age, makes full-field reads, and his ball comes out clean from the platform. Arm talent is plus — he can drive the 18-yard out from the far hash without overstriding. Mechanics are coached-up and consistent.

Strengths: Pre-snap diagnosis, anticipation throws, second-reaction creativity inside the pocket. Plus character and unteachable football IQ.
Weaknesses: Average athleticism for the modern position. Will not threaten defenses on designed runs. Footwork against pressure can drift.
Character: Glowing reports from coaches and teammates. Captain. 3.9 GPA. Volunteers with a youth football camp his father runs.

Recommendation: Highest-rated player in the class. Project as a multi-year starter beginning in Year 2. Sign him.

PROSPECT REPORT: Trey Aldridge
Position: Quarterback
Class: High school senior
Recruiting Tier: Gold
Scout Confidence: Low
Year-1 Projection: Redshirt

Measurables: 6'5", 225 lbs. 40 time 4.68. Vertical 36 inches.
Hometown: Tampa, Florida
High school: Berkeley Prep — split time as a junior, full-time starter as a senior. 2,800 yards passing, 540 yards rushing.

Trey is a freakish athlete with a left-handed delivery and a live arm. The physical tools rank in the top tier of the class — he can throw off-platform, escape pressure, and take the top off a defense. The body type and athleticism profile project an NFL ceiling.

Concerns are entirely off the field. Trey was suspended one game his junior season for a team-rules violation (details undisclosed by the school). His senior coach reports inconsistent practice habits and friction with position coaches. Two of his three letters of recommendation hedged on leadership. We had to push for a second visit before he committed.

Strengths: Elite arm talent, dual-threat ceiling, left-handed (rare). Very high physical ceiling.
Weaknesses: Read progression is rushed. Will lock onto first read. Footwork in muddied pockets is undisciplined.
Character: Maturity flag. Coaches Whitlock and Greene split on whether to take him. Position coach Greene is the dissent vote.

Recommendation: Sign with a clear set of academic and behavioral benchmarks. Talent worth the risk; floor is real.

PROSPECT REPORT: Khari Boyles
Position: Wide Receiver
Class: High school senior
Recruiting Tier: Blue Chip
Scout Confidence: High
Year-1 Projection: Day-One Starter

Measurables: 6'1", 188 lbs. 40 time 4.31. Vertical 39 inches. Bench (225 lbs) 12 reps.
Hometown: Long Beach, California
High school: Poly HS — 78 catches as a junior, 1,540 yards, 18 TDs. State 100m champion as a sophomore (10.41).

Khari is the fastest player in the 2027 recruiting class — his 4.31 forty was hand-timed at our June camp and confirmed at two separate combines. The track speed translates: he stacks defenders on the vertical stem and has top-shelf ball-tracking on the deep ball. He also returns kicks, where his vision and burst are special.

The track-and-field background is the differentiator. He'll be the fastest player on our roster from the day he arrives.

Strengths: Pure speed, vertical separation, kick return ability, ball tracking. Soft hands.
Weaknesses: Route tree is narrow — heavy go-ball and slant diet at the high school level. Will need development on intermediate breaking routes. Frame is lean; press coverage at the next level will be a learning curve.
Character: Quiet, coachable. Two-sport athlete teaches discipline. Letters from both his football and track coaches are strong.

Recommendation: Day-one impact as a deep threat and returner. Sign without hesitation.

PROSPECT REPORT: Caleb Reston
Position: Linebacker (MIKE)
Class: High school senior
Recruiting Tier: Gold
Scout Confidence: High
Year-1 Projection: Rotational

Measurables: 6'2", 232 lbs. 40 time 4.68. Vertical 33 inches. Bench (225 lbs) 22 reps.
Hometown: Erie, Pennsylvania
High school: Cathedral Prep — 142 tackles as a senior, 12 TFL, 4 INTs.

Caleb is a Riverton State legacy — his father, Mark Reston, was a four-year starter at middle linebacker for the Mariners from 1994 to 1997 and remains 7th on the program's all-time tackle list. The family connection is real but not the reason we're taking him; he's a top-150 player on his own merit.

His instincts are the standout trait. He diagnoses run-pass quickly and triggers downhill without hesitation. Take-on ability is plus, and he can play through traffic to make plays at the line. Coverage skills lag — he can run with tight ends but will be exposed by good receiving backs in space.

Strengths: Instincts, downhill trigger, take-on, leadership.
Weaknesses: Coverage range. Hip flexibility in space.
Character: Captain, 3.7 GPA, deeply tied to the program through family.

Recommendation: Sign. Profiles as our future MIKE — three-year starter projection beginning Year 2.

CLASS SUMMARY

Twenty signees. Four Blue Chip prospects (Harlan, Boyles, Pruitt, Goff), eight Gold, six Silver, two Bronze. Highest-confidence player in the class is Devon Mahalu (the JUCO transfer). Highest-ceiling player is Lincoln Goff. Best value is Wyatt Connors. Best story is Saint-Joseph Okafor. The sleeper to watch is Reggie Whitten.

Position grades: Quarterback room A-, Receiver room A, Running back room B+, Tight end room C+, Offensive line A-, Defensive line B+, Linebacker room A, Secondary B, Specialists B.

Overall class grade: A-. Best class since 2019.
